Chassis

Our robot uses a custom chassis manufactured with carbon fibre to allow for customizability while maintaining rigidity. The lightweight material in combination with a 500 RPM drivetrain balanced agility and strength, being able to score elements quickly and withstand interferences. Each outer plate had material removed in a specific way to further reduce weight while maintaining strength where needed.

Intake

The intake was designed with robustness in mind. It uses custom developed vector and silicone wheels to suit our specific needs. The vector wheels funnel balls towards the centre of the robot, even against walls and corners. Once the balls are inside the robot, the transfer system takes over. It utilizes surgical tubing and back wheels to remove dead spots and provide constant force to every ball. Every step of the process is controlled by a software system utilizing multiple sensors to prevent jamming and ensure smooth ball flow. It automatically disables the intake when at max capacity to prevent fouls.

Shooter

To maximize maneuverability on the field, we built a 360 degree turret allowing us to aim from anywhere. Our turret uses a bearing stack to be thin yet sturdy, powered by 2 Axon Mini servos for high speed with full rotational range. The shooter has an adjustable hood to control the projectile trajectory, and 2 bare motors to keep a small form-factor and high velocities. Roughly 450g of steel flywheels are attached to increase the moment of inertia and shooting consistency. Each component is controlled using advanced software programs which use physics equations and closed loop controllers, maximizing accuracy and efficiency. The entire targeting system is autonomously controlled without human input for ease of driving.
